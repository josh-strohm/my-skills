# LinkedIn Post Templates & Hook Library

Load this file when you need concrete starting points: hook examples, post scaffolds, or document carousel frameworks. These are scaffolds, not formulas. The best posts break structure when the story demands it.

## Hook Library

The first 150 characters (before "See more") determine whether the post gets read. Hooks should raise a question the reader needs answered. Genuine curiosity, not clickbait.

### The Contrarian Hook
State something that goes against conventional wisdom in your space.
- "Nobody talks about this part of running an AI consulting business."
- "Cold outreach is dead. Not because it doesn't work, but because everyone is doing it wrong."
- "I turned down a $4K/mo retainer last week. Here's why that was the right call."
- "The best decision I made for my business was posting on LinkedIn with 0 followers."

### The Specific Moment Hook
Anchor to a time, place, or specific event.
- "Three years ago today, I was sitting in my car outside a client meeting, trying to figure out if I could actually make this work."
- "Last Tuesday a plumber told me he loses 30% of his calls to voicemail. That's the moment this business clicked for me."
- "I sent my first invoice in March 2024. It was for $400. I was terrified to hit send."

### The Vulnerability Hook
Admit something most people in your position wouldn't.
- "I'm 6 months into building my consulting business and honestly, some weeks I still feel like I have no idea what I'm doing."
- "I've had two clients churn this year. I'm going to tell you exactly why, because I think most of it was my fault."
- "My first three LinkedIn posts were terrible. I deleted them. But I learned what actually works from writing them."

### The Data Hook
Lead with a number that surprises.
- "60% of missed calls to trades businesses go to voicemail. Each one is a job that went to a competitor."
- "I had 0 followers and 0 connections when I started posting. 90 days later I've booked 4 calls from LinkedIn. None from ads."
- "A single missed call costs a trades business an average of $300-500 in lost revenue."

### The List Hook
Promise specific takeaways.
- "5 things I would do differently in my first 90 days as a solo AI consultant."
- "3 types of trades businesses that benefit most from AI receptionists. (And one that definitely shouldn't bother.)"

### The Question Hook
Ask the thing everyone is thinking.
- "Why do so many trades businesses still use a single line phone for everything?"
- "Is anyone actually making money from AI yet, or is it all hype and course-sellers?"

### Hook types to avoid
- "Excited to share..." (LinkedIn cliche, instant scroll)
- "I'm thrilled to announce..." (press release voice)
- "Here are 5 ways to..." (overused list-icle hook)
- "Let's talk about..." (signposting, AI tell)
- "In today's..." / "In the world of..." / "Navigating the..." (AI boilerplate)
- Any hook that promises something the post doesn't deliver

## Post Templates

### Template 1: The Lesson Post
A single insight drawn from a specific moment in your work.

```
<Hook: contrarian, vulnerable, or specific moment>

[1-2 short paragraphs setting the scene. Be specific. Name the client, the situation, the moment.]

[The turn: what happened next, or what you realized.]

[The lesson, stated plainly. No hedging.]

[Optional: one specific thing you changed as a result.]

<CTA: question or invitation to comment>
```

### Template 2: The Unpopular Opinion Post
State your position and defend it hard.

```
<Hook: your contrarian position, stated boldly>

[I believe X. Most people in <space> believe Y. They're wrong, and here's why.]

[2-3 short paragraphs defending your position with specifics: a story, a data point, a client example.]

[The nuance: acknowledge the part where Y is actually right. This is what makes it feel human.]

<CTA: am I wrong on this?>
```

### Template 3: The How-To / Framework Post
A specific process you actually use.

```
<Hook: the outcome and that you'll share the method>

[Quick framing of the problem this solves. 2-3 lines max.]

[Steps in plain language. No jargon. Short paragraphs.]

[Why this works: the principle behind the steps.]

[One specific result from using this: a number, a client outcome, a before/after.]

<CTA: comment if you want me to send the detailed version>
```

### Template 4: The Story Post
A narrative. A specific moment that taught you something.

```
<Hook: the moment, anchored in time and place>

[I was <doing something> when <something happened>.]

[The tension. What was at stake.]

[The choice you made.]

[What happened next. Be precise.]

[What you learned. One sentence. Direct.]

<Optional CTA: has something like this happened to you?>
```

### Template 5: The Callout Post
Highlight a person, product, or business you genuinely admire.

```
<Hook: the person/thing you want to shout out and why>

[What they did that impressed you. Be specific.]

[Why you think this matters in <your space>.]

[Optional: the broader lesson.]

<CTA: who has impressed you lately?>
```

### Template 6: The Reaction Post
Your take on a piece of news, a thread, or content from someone else.

```
<Hook: the take, attributed to the source>

[What they said. Briefly.]

[Where you agree.]

[Where you disagree or want to push further.]

[What you'd add: the specific angle that nobody else is talking about.]

<CTA: what do you think?>
```

### Template 7: The Honest Update Post
A real progress update on something you're building.

```
<Hook: the thing you're building and where you are now>

[What you said you'd do last month/quarter/year.]

[What actually happened. Be honest. Including the misses.]

[One thing you learned that surprised you.]

[What you're trying next.]

<CTA: if you've done something similar, what worked for you?>
```

## Document Post (PDF Carousel) Framework

Document posts have the highest engagement on LinkedIn (6.6% average, 278% more than video). Use them when you have a framework or list worth dwelling on.

**Format:** 5-10 slides. 1080x1080px square. One idea per slide. Export as PDF.

**Slide structure:**
1. **Hook slide** - The promise. Big text. "5 mistakes I made on my first 3 client calls."
2. **Slides 2 through n-1** - One idea per slide. Brief. Specific. Visual.
3. **Closing slide** - The CTA. "Comment 'mistakes' and I'll send you the full breakdown."

**What works:**
- Step-by-step frameworks with actual client examples
- Before/after transformations with numbers
- Industry benchmarks and data visualizations
- Career lessons and insights
- Myth-busting with evidence

**What doesn't:**
- Slide after slide of generic advice ("Be clear in your messaging" tells nobody anything)
- More than 10 slides (engagement drops)
- Wall-of-text slides (one sentence per slide max)
- Polished but soulless content. People scroll past it.

## Content Calendar Framework

For a week of posting (3-5 posts), vary the type:

| Day | Post type | Hook style | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | Lesson from the week | Contrarian or specific moment | Text |
| Tuesday | Framework / how-to | Data or list | Document carousel |
| Thursday | Story / opinion | Vulnerability or contrarian | Text or native video |
| Friday | Callout or reaction | Question | Text |

Rules:
- 24 hours minimum between posts
- No more than 2 text posts in a row
- Mix hook types across the week (not all contrarian, not all vulnerability)
- 3-5 hashtags per post, varied but relevant

## What NOT to do

- Don't post AI-slop. The algorithm signals down content that reads as AI-generated. More importantly, humans scroll past it.
- Don't include external links in posts. ~60% reach penalty. The "comment for link" workaround is also penalized as of early 2026.
- Don't post more than once in 24 hours. Back-to-back penalty up to 20%.
- Don't post fewer than 3 times a week. Consistency matters more than volume.
- Don't write hooks that make promises the post doesn't keep. Once you lose trust you don't get it back.
- Don't use emojis as bullet points or section markers. Major AI tell.
- Don't write in the second person ("you need to") for the whole post. Mix in first person.
- Don't make every post a "lesson." Some can be questions, some reactions, some callouts. Variety signals human.
- Don't use broetry formatting (one sentence per line, every line) as your default. Use it intentionally for emphasis only.
- Don't stuff hashtags. 3-5 max. More signals spam.
